Proper & Improper Fractions
All Answers
Class Work · Part A — Proper & Improper Fractions (Questions 1–10)
1
Sort: 3/5, 7/5, 2/2, 1/9, 10/3 into Proper or Improper.
▶
✓ Proper fractions (top < bottom): 3/5, 1/9

✓ Improper fractions (top ≥ bottom): 7/5 (7>5), 2/2 (2=2), 10/3 (10>3)
⚡ When the top equals the bottom (like 2/2), it means 1 whole — still counted as improper because the top is NOT less than the bottom.
2
Shade 5/4 using two rectangle bars.
▶
Draw TWO rectangles, each divided into 4 equal parts. Total parts shaded = 4 + 1 = 5 parts. This confirms 5/4 = 1 whole and 1 extra quarter.
5/4 = 1¼ as a mixed number.
3
Convert 9/4 to a mixed number.
▶
Step 1: 9 ÷ 4 = 2 remainder 1
Step 2: 2 whole groups of 4, with 1 left over
Step 3: Write it → Whole = 2, leftover fraction = 1/4

✓ 9/4 = 2¼
Check: 2 × 4 + 1 = 9 ✓. Always check by working backwards!
4
Convert 2¼ to an improper fraction.
▶
Step 1: Whole number = 2, denominator = 4
Step 2: 2 × 4 = 8
Step 3: 8 + 1 (the numerator) = 9
Step 4: Keep denominator → 9/4

✓ 2¼ = 9/4
5
Mum bought 11 slices. Each loaf has 6 equal slices. Write as an improper fraction and mixed number.
▶
Improper fraction: 11 slices out of groups of 6 → 11/6

Converting: 11 ÷ 6 = 1 remainder 5
→ 1 whole loaf and 5 slices left

✓ 11/6 = 1⅚
Mum bought almost 2 full loaves but not quite.
6
Circle the improper fractions: 4/7, 8/8, 3/10, 5/2, 6/9, 11/11.
▶
✓ Improper fractions: 8/8 (8=8), 5/2 (5>2), 11/11 (11=11)

Not improper: 4/7 (4<7 ✗), 3/10 (3<10 ✗), 6/9 (6<9 ✗)

3 improper fractions found.
7
Convert 13/5 to a mixed number and mark on a number line.
▶
Step 1: 13 ÷ 5 = 2 remainder 3
Step 2: 2 whole groups + 3/5 left over
✓ 13/5 = 2⅗

Number line: Mark a point between 2 and 3, closer to 3 (since 3/5 > ½).
8
Write a proper fraction and an improper fraction both with denominator 7.
▶
Sample answer:
Proper fraction with denominator 7: 3/7 (any fraction where top < 7)
Improper fraction with denominator 7: 9/7 (any fraction where top ≥ 7)

Other correct examples: 2/7 and 8/7  |  5/7 and 7/7  |  1/7 and 11/7
Full marks for any correct pair with denominator 7, plus matching shade diagrams.
9
Tayo says "6/6 is a proper fraction because the numbers are the same." Is Tayo right?
▶
✗ Tayo is WRONG.

6/6 is an improper fraction (not proper). A proper fraction needs the top to be smaller than the bottom. When the top equals the bottom (like 6/6), the fraction equals exactly 1 whole — it is not less than 1, so it cannot be proper.

6/6 = 1 whole.
Rule: Proper → top STRICTLY less than bottom. If top = bottom or top > bottom → improper.
10
BOSS: A recipe needs 3½ cups. Write as an improper fraction. How many ½-cup scoops?
▶
Step 1 — Convert 3½ to improper:
3 × 2 = 6, then 6 + 1 = 7  →  7/2

Step 2 — How many ½-cup scoops in 7/2?
7/2 ÷ 1/2 = 7/2 × 2/1 = 7
OR: Each scoop = ½ cup; 3½ ÷ ½ = 7

✓ The cook needs 7 scoops.
Shortcut check: 3½ cups × 2 scoops-per-cup = 7 scoops ✓
